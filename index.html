About this song

Release Date: January 6, 2025
Lyrics: Kako, WOONHAK
Composer: Pop Time, Kako, Ryo, ZICO, TAESAN, WOONHAK
Arranger: Pop Time, Daily, Likey
Producer: Pop Time, Kako
Source: KOZ Entertainment

Described as fresh but relatable by the members, "If I Say, I Love You" is an energetic track with realistic lyrics about suffering from a breakup. The song lyrics focuses on the emotion of 'patheticness', a self-deprecating portrayal after a breakup. Its catchy rhythm and vibrant music video showcase the group's charming and relatable storytelling.

Analysis

The song is written from the point of view of a boy experiencing the aftermath of a breakup. The intro down to the first verse introduces us to the brokenhearted state of this boy.

This is just what I'm like ever since that day
Can't even pick up the guitar anymore
Yeah, it's just what I'm like ever since that day
It's funny but

“That day” could refer to the day of the break up. He says he is the same as that day in a way that he is still heartbroken, to the point that he can't even pick up the guitar anymore.

As this verse plays, the music video shows Taesan, the main protagonist, joined with the other group members who represent his other personalities whom he made as a way to cope with his heartbreak. Different scenes in the music video continuously hint at this narrative.

The six personalities represented by each member.

When Jaehyun is behind the camera, but the face captured is Woonhak.

The other members moving as one in each television.

The members yawning at the same time right after Leehan.

The members popping out behind Riwoo in the photo booth.

Them being with the same girl as the same place.

Dirty laundry's overflowing in the washer
Hair's sticking up, it's a mess
Outside's bustling, even cherry blossoms look busy but not me

The first passage of the verse shows how unkempt and detached he has been.

It's just pathetic
Yeah, I've got it bad
It's not like tomorrow
I'll wake up as a brand new person
And to use my memories
To write another song
There's nothing I hate more than that

The second talks about how pathetic he feels as he is still hung up on the girl, and how much he hates that he is writing another song about her using his memories.

This is just what I'm like ever since that day
Can't even pick up the guitar anymore
Yeah, it's just what I'm like ever since that day
It's funny but
If I say I love you 愛してる 사랑해 just for today
Knowing I'd regret it, would it be better that way?
Love you

The chorus talks about how he considers whether or not he should confess even if he would regret it. This regret could refer to the heartbreak he would go through from their break up.

A small detail that could show just how much he truly loves this girl is in the way he says “I love you” in English, Japanese, and Korean. An added bonus is that the “I love you”s play at the 1:43 mark of the music video—this number, too, means “I love you” in slang.

Wanna tell ya
I could fake the inspiration
And write a lyric that's so cliché but
In the end, it all comes back to you
So sick of being sober
Pretending this isn't a song about you
I'll just keep on living like that, today, tomorrow, on and on
But I'm so sick of it

The second verse seems to be a denial of how he still loves the girl. All the lyrics he writes are still about her. He does this even when sober, hence, “So sick of being sober”. The verse ends with “But I'm so sick of it” as if to say, he doesn't want to keep pretending that their remaining feelings don't exist. The music video aligns with this as he is shown holding a book, entitled, “Say It”.

To be honest, music is nothing but
One big emotional black hole
Writing songs, playing guitar, it all feels meaningless
Like I'm forcing a feeling, it all feels phony
So I'm done
What’s it matter anyway

The bridge seems to be about how he feels about music now—inauthentic, forced, and meaningless—now that they are no longer together.

But why is it that Taesan is only about to confess in the music video? The theory is that the whole video is just a reminiscence of the day he confesses to the girl, meaning it is set in the past.

Tripping on someone, approaching the wrong girl, getting hit on the head with a snowball, then fainting from the impact—it started with Taesan fumbling his confession. This could be his imagination of the situation in a way so that he wouldn't be able to confess—a way of coping with the heartbreak, like a wish for the confession to not have happened at all.

However, in the final scene, we return to the beginning sequence of the video, only this time, he goes for the right girl. The line “What’s it matter anyway” is heard before he starts running with a determined expression on his face, a representation that even if he knows he'll regret confessing because of the heartbreak he would later experience, he'll still do it anyway.

His feelings are confessed through the yellow flower concealed and preserved by the coolness of the snowball, possibly a representation of the love he continued to have for the girl and how precious these feelings are to him still.

The video ends with split-second shots of the members, a final detail solidifying the fact that the group members are indeed, one person.
